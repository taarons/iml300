Website Critique

Layout

1. Good Layout

I chose damndelicious as my good example because A) it's a cooking website which requires a space as visually appealing as possible in order to intice use and B) it's instructional so it needs to have a very functional and readable quality to it. The homepage maintains consistent image sizes as well as proportional font sizing and spacing in my opinion.

2. Bad Typography

One of the key points I understood from reading up on vertical rhythm was the importance of proportional values in regards to line-height and spacing. To find a bad example, I decided to look at old MySpace pages which gave users arguably too much freedom to manipulate their interface without really maintaining any proportional consistencies.
